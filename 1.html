6 New Organizations Establish in QSHS

Marl Gabriel M. Decuzar

Quezon Science High School forms brand new clubs to allow students in showcasing their talents and to interact with other levels.

The newly-formed clubs are the Engineering club named RobITech, Socratics or the Philosophy club, Speaker’s club, AP club nicknamed Blitzkrieg, the Sports club also known as Athlos, and the Filipino club or the ‘Samahang Angat Wika’.

Athlos held their first project which is the Sports Tournament wherein three games are made available to play which had a small fee and the served as the foundation of the club.

Basketball, table tennis and chess are the sports that are talked about to be played in the tournament and are scheduled to start after classes.

But before this tournament, the club has already thought of this as a replacement for the Intramurals due to the controversies.

Speaker’s club plans to compete in UP for a contest in debates on the month of February although the trip is tentative because of the problems it has to face for it being an outside the province event.

“Hindi kami ganoon kasigurado kung makakapunta pati hindi na rin kami makapagpraktis dahil nga hindi kami binibigyan ng permission ng Rotary Club”, said Irish Gagalang, president of the Speaker’s club.

Blitzkrieg is the chosen name for the AP club. The name is taken from the tactic Hitler used during the Second World War and the word meant “lightning war”.

The club also facilitated the Martial Law commemoration day which is the first in the school which conducted a few competitions as a part of the said event.

“Besides the Martial Law event, we have more projects to be done especially the AP camp which is coming really soon and the Cultural Fest, proposed to be done in November”, said Ed Lorenz Ladines, a Grade 8- representative of the AP club.

Engineering club also known as RobITech is established to help students learn more about technology and in applied science used in the investigative projects.

Ian Salazar, president of the club said that he wishes to bring the students’ understanding about the technology around us higher through seminars and practical application.

The Philosophy club named Socratics has proposed a number of activities for the year like a welcome party, an everyday meditation and many more but the latest one is “Tintahan”.

“Tintahan” is an event where people gather in a place where they could gather and appreciate different forms of art like singing, dancing and more which is relaxing and removes the stress in the mind.

Samahang-angat wika or the Filipino club is an organization that should have been established long ago but has only debuted this school year.

Aileen Marie Villarama has been chosen as the club’s adviser and is also a teacher in the subject.

Although some of these clubs aren’t really approved by the directress and can also be considered as independent clubs, all of these clubs wish to help all students through interactions between members and increase the knowledge of each individual by joining these kind of organizations.
